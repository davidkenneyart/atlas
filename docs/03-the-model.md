# The Model

Atlas is built from a small number of simple concepts.

The Core remains intentionally small. Everything else is built upon it.

As a result, Atlas is easy to begin with, yet capable of describing knowledge of almost any complexity.

---

# Core

The Core of Atlas consists of just three concepts.

## Thing

Everything begins with a Thing.

A Thing is anything that exists and has meaning to the person building their knowledge.

A Thing might be a work, a person, a place, an idea, a project, a document, an exhibition or something entirely different.

If it matters to you, it can exist as a Thing.

Every Thing has a unique identity within Atlas.

How people choose to describe that Thing is entirely their own.

---

## Attribute

Attributes describe Things.

An Attribute stores information about a Thing.

Examples include:

* Name
* Title
* Description
* Date
* Material
* Location
* Dimensions

Different Things may have different Attributes.

Atlas does not require every Thing to share the same information.

A painting may have a Medium.

A person may have a Name.

A place may have Coordinates.

An idea may simply have a short description.

Attributes describe what is known.

They do not determine what a Thing is.

---

## Connection

Connections describe relationships between Things.

Examples include:

* Part of
* Inspired by
* Created by
* Located in
* Exhibited at

Knowledge becomes more valuable as Things become connected.

Connections are the foundation of discovery within Atlas.

---

# Modelling

The Core is sufficient to describe knowledge.

Modelling makes that knowledge easier to organise, understand and extend.

## Types

Types group similar Things together.

Examples include:

* Work
* Person
* Project
* World
* Narrative
* Exhibition

Types are optional.

A Thing can exist without a Type.

Types reduce repetition, provide structure and make larger collections easier to manage.

Types may suggest common Attributes and Connections, while remaining entirely configurable by the person building the model.

---

## Profiles

Profiles package modelling decisions for particular domains.

A Creative Practice profile might define common Types such as Works, Projects and Exhibitions.

A Museum profile may define Objects, Collections and Acquisitions.

Profiles provide sensible starting points.

They never limit what Atlas can become.

---

# Discovery

Atlas stores truth.

It derives understanding.

## Paths

Paths are discovered through Connections.

They reveal relationships that were never entered directly.

A Work connected to a Narrative, connected to a World, is also connected to that World through a Path.

---

## Patterns

Patterns are observations.

They emerge from the Things, Attributes and Connections that already exist.

Examples include:

* Most Works use concrete.
* Most Projects relate to a particular World.
* Studies frequently become Works.

Patterns are never entered by the person using Atlas.

They are observed by Atlas.

---

# In Summary

Atlas stores only three things:

* Things
* Attributes
* Connections

Everything else emerges.

Types help people model knowledge.

Paths reveal structure.

Patterns reveal behaviour.

Understanding belongs to the person.
