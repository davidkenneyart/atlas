# 00 · Vision

## A system for building understanding.

Knowledge is rarely created all at once.

It grows through ideas, experiments, conversations, observations, failures and finished works. Over time these become connected, forming a body of knowledge that is often richer and more complex than any individual can comfortably hold in their mind.

Most software helps people store information.

Atlas exists to help people understand it.

Atlas begins with a simple idea.

Meaningful structures emerge by connecting simple Things together.

Rather than asking people to design databases, schemas or hierarchies before they begin, Atlas encourages them to begin with something that already exists.

A sketch.

A note.

A photograph.

A work.

A place.

A person.

An idea.

Create a Thing.

Connect it.

Build understanding.

Create another Thing.

Connect it.

Continue building.

As the graph grows, so does understanding.

Atlas does not impose structure.

It allows structure to emerge.

Atlas does not invent meaning.

It reveals meaning that already exists within the relationships people create.

Every Thing is equally welcome.

An unfinished sketch, a conversation, a work of art, a research note, a photograph, a person, a place or an idea all have value.

Their importance emerges through how they connect to everything else.

Atlas stores knowledge once.

Everything else is a way of seeing it.

The canvas.

The inspector.

Search.

Publishing.

Discovery.

Each is simply a different perspective on the same underlying graph.

Atlas invites play.

It encourages curiosity, experimentation and discovery.

It should be easy to begin, easy to change direction and easy to rebuild.

Mistakes are expected.

Exploration is encouraged.

Understanding emerges through making.

Atlas is intentionally platform-independent.

Its ideas are not tied to any particular technology or programming language.

WordPress is the first reference implementation, not the definition of the project.

Atlas is for anyone building a connected body of knowledge.

Artists, writers, researchers, historians, museums, architects, designers, product teams and countless others share the same challenge:

understanding how individual pieces form a meaningful whole.

Atlas exists to help people see what they have built.

Not by replacing human thinking,

but by extending it.

---

## Vision

To create an open, enduring system that helps people build understanding by connecting the Things they create.

---

## Guiding Beliefs

**Making is thinking.**

Understanding does not precede creation.

It emerges through it.

**The graph is the truth.**

Everything else is a way of seeing it.
