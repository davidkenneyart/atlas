# Atlas

> **Atlas is a construction system for connected knowledge.**

Knowledge doesn't exist in isolation.

Ideas become works.
Works become projects.
Projects become exhibitions.
Exhibitions become histories.

The value isn't in the individual pieces.

It's in the connections between them.

Atlas is an open-source project exploring a different way of organising knowledge.

Instead of asking people to design databases, schemas or folder structures before they begin, Atlas starts with a single idea:

> **Create something.**

Connect it.

Keep making.

Structure emerges through making.

Meaning emerges through connection.

---

## Why?

Most software asks people to organise information.

Atlas helps people understand it.

Whether you're an artist, researcher, museum curator, architect, writer, or anyone building a connected body of knowledge, Atlas is designed to reveal relationships that become difficult to see as your work grows.

Atlas doesn't invent meaning.

It reveals it.

---

## Principles

* Things come before categories.
* Connections matter more than hierarchy.
* Structure emerges through making.
* Organisation should invite play.
* Discovery should reward curiosity.
* Mistakes should be inexpensive.
* Atlas suggests. People decide.

---

## Philosophy

We believe that **making is thinking**.

Understanding rarely appears fully formed.

It emerges through experimentation, iteration and connection.

Atlas is designed to support that process.

Like building with Lego, Atlas should invite people to create, connect, reshape and explore without fear of making mistakes.

---

## Current Status

Atlas is currently in the design phase.

The philosophy, ontology and architecture are being developed in public before implementation begins.

The first reference implementation will be built for WordPress, but Atlas is being designed as a platform-independent model rather than a WordPress-specific tool.

---
